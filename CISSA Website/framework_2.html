Stuxnet

The Stuxnet attack, widely publicized in 2010, is arguably the most well-known example of a cyber attack targeting systems compromising of both cyber and physical equipment. The attack was targeted at the centrifuge machinery in the Iranian nuclear enrichment facilities. This worm works its attack in multiple steps, with each increasing the potential of damage incurred. The attack first enters into a cyber system via public Internet and escalated its privilege similar to an ordinary cyber attack. This step involves multiple zero-day exploits. It then attempts to bypass the "air-gap" that is supposed to physically segregate the control networks from the Internet by infecting USB storage devices. When certain conditions are met and the network in within the attacker's control, it intermittently changes the spinning speed of the centrifuges. As a result, the Stuxnet worm has damaged Iran's centrifuges and delayed its uranium enrichment efforts.
